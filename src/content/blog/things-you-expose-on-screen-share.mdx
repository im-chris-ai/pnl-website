You hit "Share screen" and the page you're on is suddenly in front of everyone.

Most of it is fine. But web pages are packed with private stuff you stopped noticing months ago. And the second you share, it's all on display.

Here are seven things that live right on the page, the ones that catch people out most.

## 1. Account balances and financial dashboards

You pull up your analytics to show the team, and your Stripe revenue is sitting one panel over. Or your bank balance. Or this month's ad spend.

![A payments dashboard with account balance, revenue, and customer names and emails all visible](/images/blog/shared/click-to-censor/finance-dashboard.webp)

Numbers you really don't want a client, a candidate, or a webinar crowd doing math on.

The fix? Hover, click, done. Balances blacked out, customer names blurred, before you ever hit share.

![The same payments dashboard with the balance and revenue figures blacked out and customer names and emails blurred](/images/blog/shared/click-to-censor/finance-dashboard-censored.webp)

## 2. Customer records in a CRM or admin panel

Real names, emails, account IDs of actual people.

![A CRM customer list showing customer names, emails, companies, plans, and revenue](/images/blog/shared/click-to-censor/crm-customers.webp)

Demoing your product on live customer data is one of the easiest ways to leak PII without meaning to. That's not just awkward, it can be a compliance problem.

One pass and the people are anonymous: names and emails blurred, the records you're actually demoing left alone.

![The same CRM customer list with every customer name and email blurred](/images/blog/shared/click-to-censor/crm-customers-censored.webp)

## 3. Your webmail inbox

Gmail or Outlook open in a tab is a wall of private context.

Sender names. Subject lines. That one thread you'd rather nobody read over your shoulder. All sitting there while you "just quickly check something."

## 4. API keys and tokens on a settings page

You open a cloud console or a settings page to show a config, and a live API key is right there in plain text.

One screenshot from someone on the call and that key is out. For good.

## 5. Personal details on account or profile pages

Your address. Your phone number. The plan you're on, the last four of a card.

Account and profile pages love to lay all of this out in one tidy block. Great for you, great for everyone watching too.

## 6. A shared doc or spreadsheet

You open a Google Doc or Sheet to reference one line, and the whole thing is other people's info: salaries, emails, notes that were never meant for this audience.

## 7. Whatever's in the web app you're demoing

Form fields half-filled with real data. A test record that isn't actually a test. Comments, names, internal notes.

When you're live in an app, the sensitive bits are mixed right in with the thing you're trying to show.

## What it can't catch (handle these first)

Quick honesty: Click to Censor works *on the web page*. So a few things are out of its reach, and you fix those with a habit before you share:

- **Notifications.** Flip on Do Not Disturb (or Focus, or Quiet Hours).
- **Your tab strip and bookmarks bar.** Close the spare tabs, hide the bookmarks bar.
- **Browser autofill dropdowns.** Those pop from the browser, not the page.
- **Files in your editor or terminal.** A `.env` open in your code editor isn't a web page at all, so close it.

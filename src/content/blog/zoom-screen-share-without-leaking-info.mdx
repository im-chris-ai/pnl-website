## The two ways Zoom shares leak

There are really only two.

**What Zoom shows.** The whole desktop instead of one window. A notification that pops mid-share. The taskbar full of app icons you'd rather not explain.

**What's on the page.** Even with the right window shared, the page itself is full of private stuff: balances, customer names, your open inbox, an API key on a settings screen.

Zoom can fix the first one. It can't touch the second. So you handle them separately.

## Lock down what Zoom shares

This part is all in Zoom's own controls. Two minutes, no extensions.

**Share one window, not your whole screen.** When you click Share, Zoom gives you a grid of open windows. Pick the single app you actually need. Everything else, your desktop, your other tabs, your inbox, stays invisible. This one habit prevents most leaks on its own.

![A screen-share window picker with one app window selected and the entire-screen, inbox, and messages options flagged as private](/images/blog/shared/click-to-censor/zoom-share-picker.webp)

See how "Entire Screen" hands over everything at once? Sharing a single window keeps the rest of your desktop private by default.

**Silence notifications while sharing.** Zoom has a setting for this (Settings > Share Screen > "Silence system notifications when sharing desktop"). Turn it on. Or just flip your OS into Do Not Disturb / Focus before the call. No more DMs surfacing on the projector.

**Watch the green border.** When you share, Zoom draws a green outline around exactly what's being broadcast. Glance at it before you start talking. If it's hugging your whole screen, stop and re-share just the window.

**Tidy the window you are sharing.** Close the spare tabs in that browser. Hide the bookmarks bar. Quit the apps you don't need.

That covers the *frame*. But the content inside the frame is still wide open.

## Now hide what's on the page

Here's the part people miss.

You did everything right: shared one browser window, silenced notifications. And that window is your analytics dashboard with this month's revenue sitting in the corner. Or a CRM full of real customer emails. Sharing the right window doesn't make the data on it private.

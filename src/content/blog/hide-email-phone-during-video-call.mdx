## Where your email and phone actually hide

You'd be surprised how many places leak these two.

**Your inbox.** Gmail or Outlook shows your address in the top corner, and again on every message you've sent.

**Account and profile pages.** The tidy little block with your name, email, phone, and address. Great for you, great for everyone watching.

**Email signatures.** Open a compose window to reference something and your full contact card is right there at the bottom.

**Autofill and form fields.** A checkout or a sign-up form half-filled with your real details.

**Anywhere you're logged in.** Half the apps you use greet you with "signed in as your@email.com" somewhere on screen.

Any one of these is a screenshot away from a stranger's notes.

## Why "just be careful" doesn't work

The usual advice is to close everything sensitive first. Sure.

But you'll miss one. You always do. The email in the corner is so familiar you stopped seeing it months ago.

You could blur your camera background, but that does nothing for what's on the shared screen. You could crop the window, except the private bit is usually right next to the thing you came to show.

And covering it with a sticky note or another window looks unprofessional and slips the moment you scroll.

The real problem: your email and phone are *on the page*. So the fix has to be on the page too.

Click to Censor runs **100% locally** in your browser. Your email and phone never get uploaded, there's no account, no server sees the page. It's just hiding pixels on your own screen.

## What it can't reach (handle these first)

Quick honesty: it works *on the web page*. A few leaks live outside the page, so fix those with a habit before you share:

- **Notifications.** A text preview with your number can pop mid-call. Turn on Do Not Disturb.
- **Browser autofill dropdowns.** Those render from the browser, not the page, so don't click into an email field on the call.
- **Your email in a native app.** Outlook or Mail as a desktop app isn't a web page. Close it or hide the window.

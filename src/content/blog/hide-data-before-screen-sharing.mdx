Sharing your screen is a small leap of faith.

One stray notification, one tab you forgot, and a client's email or your bank balance is suddenly on display for the whole call. I've done it. Once was enough.

## The problem

Your screen holds more private stuff than you think.

Emails. Account balances. Customer names in a dashboard. API keys in a terminal. A half-written DM that just slid in. On a normal day, fine. The second you share your screen or hit record, all of it is fair game.

And you can't take it back. Once it's on the call or baked into the video, it's out there.

## Why the usual fixes fall short

So what do most people do? They scramble.

- Close every tab and app first. You'll miss one.
- Crop or edit the recording later. Slow, and no help on a live call.
- Just hope nobody notices. Bold.

They all rely on you remembering, under pressure, in real time. That's exactly when you forget.

## The fast way

Here's what I do instead: hide the sensitive stuff right on the page, before I share.
